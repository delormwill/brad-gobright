Brad Gobright

1988 - 2019

June 16, 1988 - November 27, 2019

Gobright was a true climber’s climber; a sweet, irreverent, and charismatic figure who deserved the badge of being a true dirtbag. - Outside Online

Hardest-Graded Climbs

Redpoint: 5.14 (8b+)

Bouldering: V10 (7C+)

A timeline of Brad's life

  • 1988 - Born in Orange County, California
  • 1994 - Brad begins climbing at the age of six
  • 2009 - Drops out of college and pursues rock climbing full time, picking up odd jobs during the winter
  • 2015 - Brad and Mason Earle make the first free ascent of The Heart Route on El Capitan
  • 2016 - He and Scott Bennett climb three routes on El Capitan in 24 hours - Zodiac, The Nose, and Lurking Fear
  • 2017 - Safety Third, a 27-minute documentary about Brad, is included in Reel Rock 12. The film showcases his trad and free solo climbs, including his free solo ascent of Hairstyles and Attitudes on the Bastille in Colorado Canyon
  • 2017 - He and Jim Reynolds set a speed record for The Nose on El Capitan: 2:19:44. This ascent is captured in the 8-minute film Two Nineteen Forty Four
  • June 2019 - Alex Honnold and Brad make the second free ascent of El Niño on El Capitan
  • November 2019 - Brad falls to his death at the age of 31 on El Sendero Luminoso in El Protrero Chico, Mexico from a simul-rappelling accident. His climbing partner Aidan Jacobson survived the fall with injuries
